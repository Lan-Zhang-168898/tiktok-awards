The e-commerce and monetisation teams focused on separate goals—GMV growth vs. revenue and Take Rate (TR). To unlock sustainable growth, the SEA E-commerce Ads TR Improvement Project aligned both objectives into a self-reinforcing cycle: grow revenue, reinvest in users and merchants, boost GMV, and repeat. This was achieved through two major efforts: embedding ad value into the organic distribution system and fostering close cross-team collaboration. By introducing eCPM-based interventions across all traffic endpoints—from search to feeds—the project optimized recommendation logic for both user experience and platform profit. As a result, ad TR rose from 5.5% to 7.3%, while GMAX’s GMV share jumped from 32% to 49%, and income share from 70% to 88%—the fastest TR growth seen across all e-commerce platforms. Importantly, this was done without compromising GMV. In reverse experiments, ads ADVV increased by 14%, proving value-added traffic efficiency. The project also laid the groundwork for long-term system unification: organic and ads systems are now converging with shared value signals, cutting redundant development and data costs. A “shared-metrics” culture underpins this success. The organic team now owns profitability as a KPI, while the ads team incorporates GMV and user experience into its goals. Advertising has evolved into a core business lever—integrated into merchant tools, creator incentives, payment systems, and even AM performance evaluations. OKRs across functions are fully aligned. This project integrated monetisation and growth into one unified engine driving platform success.

This project was jointly driven by the Philippines Operations, regional Logistics, User Growth, and Finance teams, focusing on the outer island markets of Visayas and Mindanao. - To ensure sustainable growth, the Philippines Operations led cross-functional collaboration with Logistics, UG, and Finance to develop a negotiation strategy with logistics partner J&T. The approach centered on securing cost reductions in exchange for committed order volume. This resulted in a special pricing agreement offering discounts on incremental orders in the outer islands. - In 2025 Q4, UG and Ops teams initiated experiments to increase shipping subsidies in the developing regions, achieving a significant uplift in GMV (+3.1%) and MAC (+4.6%), albeit with a cost increase of 1pt only. Based on this logic, PH Ops organized XFN to collaborate closely and planned negotiation strategy together with Logistic team. - Right after logistic team successfully drove and concluded "volume-for-price" strategy negotiations with their great expertees, UG team run out a zero-minimum-spend free shipping strategy. This initiative fueled a +25% WoW increase in outer islands orders, significantly strengthening TTS's market position in the developing regions. In December, costs were diluted significantly, and at the same time the initiative drove strong growth in both GMV and MAC. GMV control share +13.2pt MoM, and MAC control share +9pt MoM, both hitting historical highs. - Beyond short-term business impact, this project established a scalable “volume-for-price” mechanism, paving the way for broader adoption across SEA and enabling stronger pricing negotiations in the Philippines for 2026. It exemplifies seamless cross-functional collaboration, with teams jointly owning goals and delivering amplified impact. - Through data analysis, the team identified that the key gap in GMV and MAC market share lies in the outer islands. Complemented by in-depth user research, we confirmed that lower shipping fees are the primary user demand.
