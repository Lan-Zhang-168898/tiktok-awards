The Intra-EU Program is a cross-market, cross-functional initiative that transformed TikTok Shop’s operations in Europe from fragmented, country-specific models into an integrated EU5 system. It serves as both a foundational infrastructure upgrade and a repeatable playbook for future expansion into EU20 and other multi-market regions. 1. Strategic Integration & Governance The project introduced a unified EU-level governance framework, aggregating key seller metrics and applying core strategies (AHR, SPS, Probation) across countries. This shift from country-level to region-level governance enabled platform-wide visibility and scalable seller management. 1. Scalable Product & Experience Design The team delivered 25+ features in 3 months, spanning 40+ Seller Center pages and 300+ system modules. These reusable components ensured consistent cross-border shopping, promotion, and fulfillment experiences. By July 5, intra-EU products accounted for 27% of EU5’s L2L GMV, far exceeding expectations. In some smaller markets like Spain, intra-EU GMV reached 44%, enabling fairer exposure for merchants in less mature markets. Creator adoption was equally strong, with 48% of content featuring intra-EU products, and CTR outperforming traditional L2L goods. 1. Infrastructure & Ecosystem Enablement GNE and CSM teams were instrumental in enabling this growth, through governance logic, EU-wide APIs, ISV onboarding, and key merchant support. These efforts allowed markets to rapidly scale up despite lean resources. 1. Strategic Spillover The program introduced core concepts—such as “EU as One,” B-side and C-side GMV separation, and “home vs. register country”—which have now been successfully implemented. These innovations will directly support the launch of EU20 and other small-market clusters in the future. 1. Cross-Functional Collaboration This success would not have been possible without strong partnership across Product, Strategy, GNE, Logistics, Ops, Data, Design, and Research—across both global and regional layers. Conclusion This project delivered rapid results and laid the groundwork for long-term EU integration. It exemplifies how clear strategy, deep collaboration, and scalable execution can reshape how TikTok Shop grows across borders.

Project Background and Goal * TTS UK was lagging the US in terms of T3 and T2 merchant activation rates on Shop Ads * In addition, ROI1 Ads were being phased out in favour of ROI 2 [GMV Max], which was launched with a 3-month delay in the UK vs the US * The GMPT team set an adoption goal for SMB-eligible merchants for the UK to reach parity with the US accounting for the 3-month lag. I.e., for the UK to have the same activation rate at the end of June 2025 as the US had at the end of March. Up from ~28% end of April. In addition, there was a secondary goal for T2 merchant activation. Action plan: * Segmentation of merchant groups by team responsible for the account: TTS Ops; TTS MAI; SMB AM+; SMB HVA * Set target by segment, unique to the level of interaction with merchants in the specific pool * Drive completion via segment specific levers: + TTS Ops -> Goal per category SME, as well as the TTS Shop Ads team + TTS MAI & the SMB growth team -> Missions and touchpoints launched + SMB AM+ -> Identified the remaining SMB AM+ merchants yet to activate + SMB HVA -> Overlapped with ongoing activation project of T3+ Overall, this was a true cross functional push and effort. Between TTS Ops, as well as SMB & GMPT teams. Complication: - Initial low uptake after the first month: By end of May, we only moved from ~28% to ~30% activation rate, with very low uplift. Thus, we pivoted and restructure the levers with daily standups, all parties were fully responsible and all hands on deck. E.g., even cold calling and pitching MAI merchants directly. Result end of June: * A total of 301 T3+ SMB merchants have been activated in total on GMV Max at the end of June. Bringing us to an activation rate of ~41% (+123 net activations QoQ). A few things to call out: * Activated but churned merchants account for another 47 merchants or +6% of additional activations (bringing our total QoQ activations to 170 of 47%). We have kicked off the winback programme with the SMB team to reactivate these merchants in July. * It worked out that the real US benchmark for SMB managed merchants at the end of March was 35%, which was sizably outperformed.

In the early stage of the Japan market launch, NPS feedback and operational monitoring identified a high proportion of non-localized content. A dedicated country-level initiative was established, led by GNE and BizOps, in collaboration with DS, Product, Algo, Legal, PR, GR, Advertising, and other cross-functional teams to address the issue systematically. After more than one quarter of focused governance, the share of non-localized content in total VV decreased from 27% to below 3% and remained stable; the deboost rate of newly submitted content dropped from 57% to 20%. The team categorized the issue into three key areas—non-Japanese language, foreign-context content, and “plastic Japanese”—and developed corresponding models for governance. Through continuous optimization, recall of the most complex “plastic Japanese” model improved by 21pp (67%→81.09%), while coordinated manual evaluation and algorithm refinement controlled the false positive rate at around 2%. As governance stabilized, the strategy evolved from content-level controls to more precise account-level governance, achieving over 95% accuracy. A whitelist process was established to proactively protect more than 2,500 creators. In parallel, the team partnered with PR, GR, and Legal to strengthen creator education and reduce repeated violations. Within H2, the project significantly improved content quality and user experience, while establishing a scalable governance framework that balances localization, trust, and growth, providing a replicable model for other high-sensitivity markets.

Daily effective submissions increased from 18,400 in July to 55,400 in December (3x growth). Governance and Recommendation teams reduced exposure to low-quality content, lowering EU5 low-quality share by 4.85pt to 8.54%. With the groundwork of content quality initiatives, In Q4, recommendation team prioritized high GPM and high-engagement, high-transaction content. Our reversal tests confirmed a +32% surge in EU4 video GMV, contributing a massive +15.6% incremental GMV to EU TTS. The project delivered measurable H2 growth and established a scalable mechanism aligning supply governance and traffic allocation. Teams shared ownership and collaborated efficiently to amplify impact.
